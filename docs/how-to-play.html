← Back

Overview

Qwixx is a fast dice game for 2–5 players. Everyone has a scorecard with four colored rows of numbers. On each turn one player rolls the dice and all players choose whether to mark numbers on their card. The goal is to mark as many numbers as possible to maximize your score.

The Scorecard

Your card has four rows, each with 11 numbers:

  • Red — 2 to 12 (ascending)
  • Yellow — 2 to 12 (ascending)
  • Green — 12 to 2 (descending)
  • Blue — 12 to 2 (descending)

You must always mark numbers left to right. Any numbers you skip are permanently lost — you can never go back.

Turn Structure

One player is the roller each turn. They roll 6 dice: 2 white + 1 red, 1 yellow, 1 green, 1 blue.

Each turn has two phases:

  • Phase 1 — White Sum: The two white dice are added together. All players may mark that sum on any row, or pass.
  • Phase 2 — Color Combo: Only the roller may add one white die to one colored die and mark that sum on the matching colored row, or pass.

If the roller marks nothing in either phase, they take a penalty (−5 points).

Locking a Row

The rightmost number in each row (12 for Red/Yellow, 2 for Green/Blue) can only be marked if you already have at least 5 marks in that row. When someone marks it, the row locks for all players and that colored die is removed from the game.

Game End

The game ends immediately when either:

  • 2 rows are locked (by any combination of players), or
  • A player takes their 4th penalty.

Scoring

Each row scores based on how many marks you made. More marks are worth disproportionately more — every additional mark is worth more than the last.

Marks 12345 6789101112
Points 1361015 21283645556678

Each penalty costs −5 points. Add up all four rows and subtract penalties. Highest total wins.

Controls
